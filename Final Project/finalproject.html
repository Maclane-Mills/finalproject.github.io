Origins of the War: The French and Indian War and Tax

The French and Indian War was an event that led the American Colonies down the path to revoultion. After seven years of bloody conflict the British Empire defeated the French amounting a staggering war debt. In the minds of British officals it was only fitting that Americans should pay the majority of the debt because the war was seen as taking place in their defense. This debt led to the reformation by many British officials of tax policies within the American Colonies. Enforcing tax laws, reforming colonial administration and placing troops in America led to colonial resistance. The tighting of tax laws and a stricter control of trade and navigation became the top prioty by British officals in American colonies.And by the mid-1770's American and British relations had become strained. After several attempts to voice protests by colonials the first shots of the Revolutionary war would be fired April, 19, 1775.
