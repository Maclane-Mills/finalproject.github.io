Seige of Yorktown

Late in the summer of 1781 the British entrenched their troops around Yorktown under the command of Lord General Cornawallis. Washington saw the oppurtunity and siezed it quickly moving south with his French allies. French naval ships began taking control of Chesepeake Bay this limiting the ability for reinforcements and naval assistance for the British. Washington began the cannon barrage and began capturing key portions of the British entrenchments. Evantually this led to eh surrender of Cornwallis on October 19, 1781.
